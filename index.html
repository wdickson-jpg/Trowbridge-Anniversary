Know your way around

House Guide 📋

Everything you need to know to keep the house humming. If Stan's not around to show you — this is your Stan.

  • ☕

    Coffee Maker

    Keurig is on the counter — pods are in the drawer below. For the big crew, the drip coffee maker is in the lower cabinet. Filters in the pantry.

  • 🔥

    Grill

    Propane grill is on the deck. Extra tank is in the garage. Turn off the tank valve when done — and give the grates a brush while it's still hot.

  • 🗑️

    Trash & Recycling

    Trash goes out to the bins on the side of the garage. Pickup is Tuesday morning. Recycling is the blue bin. No plastic bags in recycling!

  • 🍽️

    Dishwasher

    Please run it when it's full and unload when done. Pods are under the sink. The bottom rack sticks a little — just give it a firm pull.

  • 🚤

    Pontoon Boat

    Keys are on the hook by the back door. Check the fuel gauge before heading out. No-wake within 100 feet of shore. Life jackets must be on board for everyone.

  • 🛶

    Kayaks & Paddleboards

    Stored on the rack by the dock. Please rinse after use and put them back on the rack — not on the ground. Paddles are in the dock box.

  • 🏊

    Swimming

    Swim off the dock anytime. The swim ladder is on the left side. Kids under 12 need a buddy or an adult watching. The lake drops off about 15 feet from the dock.

  • 🎣

    Fishing

    Bass, bluegill, and pike are all in the lake. Rods are in the garage. Michigan fishing license required for ages 17+. Catch and release encouraged!

  • 🔥

    Fire Pit

    Firewood is stacked in the side yard. Kindling and lighter are in the garage. Fully extinguish fires before going to bed — hose it down, don't just let it burn out.

  • 🌿

    Lawn & Garden

    Enjoy the yard! The sprinklers are on a timer — they run early morning. If you see a garden hose running, please shut it off when done.

  • 🦟

    Bugs

    Bug spray is on the shelf in the garage. Citronella candles are in the deck storage box. Michigan mosquitoes are real — spray up at dusk!

  • 🌡️

    Thermostat

    The Nest thermostat is in the hallway. Please don't set below 68 in summer or above 72 in winter. If you're the last to leave, set it to Away mode.

  • 💧

    Water

    We're on well water — it's safe but tastes different than city water. Brita pitcher is in the fridge. Please don't run water excessively.

  • 🚿

    Hot Water

    The water heater handles about 3 showers back-to-back before needing to recover. Stagger your showers during big family weekends!

  • ⚡

    Electrical Panel

    Breaker box is in the basement utility room. If something trips, check there before calling Dad. The dock outlet is on its own breaker (#14).

  • 🍂

    Fall Closing

    Usually early October. Boat comes out, dock comes in, water lines get blown out. This is a family effort — all hands on deck (literally).

  • ❄️

    Winter

    The house can be used in winter! Heat stays at 58°F minimum. Please check that no faucets are dripping and no windows are left open.

  • 🌸

    Spring Opening

    Usually early May. Dock goes in, boat gets launched, water gets turned on and tested. Another all-family event — and usually the first grill of the year!

  • ☀️

    Peak Summer

    July 4th week is prime time. Coordinate with the family on the calendar so everyone gets lake time. Weekdays are actually the best — the lake is glass.

Know the rules

Lake Association (MSLIA) ⚓

Middle Straits Lake is managed by the Middle Straits Lake Improvement Association, founded in 1973. Here are the rules that matter most for your visit.

⚠️

No-Wake Hours — This Is the Big One

Power boating at wake speed and waterskiing are prohibited on Sundays, Memorial Day, Independence Day, and Labor Day before 11:00 AM or after 7:30 PM (Eastern Daylight Time). Violators face a $100 fine plus court costs or up to 90 days imprisonment per West Bloomfield Ordinance #88.

🚤

Slow — No Wake Near Shore

Michigan law requires slow, no-wake speed within 100 feet of any shoreline, dock, raft, swimmer, or other watercraft. This applies to all boats at all times.

🏊

Waterskiing & Towing

A spotter (in addition to the driver) is required in any boat towing a person. A 170° wide-angle rearview mirror is also required. No towing between one hour after sunset and one hour before sunrise.

🔄

Counter-Clockwise Traffic

All vessels should operate in a counter-clockwise pattern around the lake when reasonably possible. This keeps traffic flowing safely and predictably.

🧥

Life Jackets Required

All vessels must carry one USCG-approved PFD per passenger. Children under 6 in open deck areas must wear a life jacket at all times — it's Michigan law.

🔴

Personal Watercraft (Jet Skis)

No PWC operation from one hour before sunset to 8:00 AM. Stay 100 ft from docks and 150 ft behind other boats. No one under 14 may operate a PWC under any circumstances.

🍻

No Boating Under the Influence

Same as driving a car — operating a vessel while intoxicated is illegal under Michigan law. Implied consent applies. Severe fines and jail time.

😲

Wake Damage Liability

The boat owner is personally responsible for any damage to life or property caused by their vessel's wake. Keep speeds safe, especially near docks and moored boats.

🚶

Kids Operating Boats

Under 12: must be supervised by someone 16+ and max 35 HP. Ages 12–15: need an adult present or a boating safety certificate. Anyone can operate up to 6 HP.
